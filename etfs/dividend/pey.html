PEY Overview - The Invesco High Yield Equity Dividend Achievers ETF

The Invesco High Yield Equity Dividend Achievers ETF (PEY) is an Equity Dividend ETF designed to provide investors with exposure to high dividend yield equities. PEY focuses on companies that have a consistent history of increasing dividends over time, aiming to capture the potential for dividend growth in the market.

One of PEY's key strategies is its focus on High Dividend Yield, which means it targets companies with a history of paying above-average dividends relative to their share price. This strategy can appeal to investors seeking income-generating investments.

PEY's payment frequency is quarterly, offering investors regular income distributions. Its geographic focus is primarily on US domestic companies, providing exposure to the American market's dividend-paying stocks.

Investors considering PEY should note its expense ratio of 0.54%, which represents the annual fee charged by the fund. The sponsor, Invesco, is a well-known and reputable investment management firm.

Overall, PEY aims to provide investors with a balanced portfolio of high dividend yield equities, combining income potential with the stability and growth potential of dividend-paying companies.

PEY - Portfolio and Asset Allocation

The Invesco High Yield Equity Dividend Achievers ETF (PEY) offers investors exposure to a portfolio focused on high dividend yield equities. Its holdings are strategically selected to include companies with a track record of consistently increasing dividends over time, reflecting a commitment to income generation.

PEY's top holdings, such as Altria (MO) and 3M Company (MMM), are renowned for their robust dividend policies, contributing to a diversified portfolio across sectors like Consumer Defensive, Financials, and Industrials. This diversification aims to reduce sector-specific risks while maintaining a steady income stream.

Furthermore, PEY's asset allocations reflect a balanced approach with significant weightage towards defensive sectors like Utilities and Consumer Defensive, mitigating overall portfolio volatility.

Investors considering PEY should evaluate factors such as its expense ratio and performance metrics, providing insights into its cost efficiency and historical returns. Overall, PEY presents an opportunity for investors seeking a balanced portfolio of dividend-paying stocks with a focus on income generation and risk mitigation.
