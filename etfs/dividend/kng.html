KNG Explained: What It Is and Why It Matters

KNG (FT Cboe Vest S&P 500 Dividend Aristocrats Target Income ETF) tracks the FT Cboe Vest S&P 500 Dividend Aristocrats Target Income Index. This index selects dividend-paying companies from the S&P 500 that have increased dividends for at least 10 consecutive years (Dividend Aristocrats) and weights them to target a specific volatility level.

Unlike broad-market dividend ETFs, KNG is designed for investors who:

  • Prioritize dividend growth quality: Focuses exclusively on companies with a proven track record of increasing payouts.
  • Seek targeted volatility: The index construction targets a volatility level, potentially offering a smoother ride than the broader market.
  • Want income with stability: Aims to deliver regular income from financially stable companies.

KNG is managed by First Trust, offering a unique take on dividend investing with its volatility-targeting mechanism built on a quality-income foundation.

Who Should Consider KNG?

KNG is best suited for income-focused investors who prioritize stability and quality in their dividend investments. It's a good fit for those constructing a portfolio with moderate risk and a steady income stream as a core objective.

Best for: conservative-to-moderate investors seeking quality dividend income with managed risk.
Not ideal for: aggressive growth seekers or those wanting broad market exposure via a low-cost, total market ETF.
Main tradeoff: you get income and lower volatility, but potentially lower long-term growth and higher fees than plain-vanilla ETFs.

Income-Focused Retirees
