The Bottom Line Up Front

Quick take: DGRW is best for investors who want dividend growth plus a quality/profitability overlay — and value monthly income over a lower expense ratio.

DGRW (WisdomTree U.S. Quality Dividend Growth Fund) adds an extra layer of discipline on top of standard dividend growth: it screens for long-term earnings growth expectations AND return on equity AND return on assets. The result is a portfolio of dividend-paying companies that are not just growing their payouts, but demonstrating genuine business quality.

DGRW Explained: What It Is and Why It Matters

Dividend growth is good. Quality dividend growth is better. DGRW insists on both.

DGRW tracks the WisdomTree U.S. Quality Dividend Growth Index, which starts with dividend-paying U.S. companies, then filters for long-term earnings growth forecasts and ranks them by return on equity (ROE) and return on assets (ROA). Only the top-quality, dividend-growing names make the cut — and holdings are weighted by dividends paid.

The DGRW edge:

  • Quality Screen: ROE + ROA filters remove financially mediocre companies from the mix.
  • Growth + Income: Both forward earnings growth AND dividend payment are required — a rare dual requirement.
  • Monthly Distributions: Unlike most quality-growth ETFs, DGRW pays monthly — a meaningful benefit for income-focused investors.

DGRW is managed by WisdomTree, the pioneer in dividend-weighted factor strategies.
