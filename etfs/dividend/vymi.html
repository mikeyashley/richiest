VYMI Overview - The Vanguard International High Dividend Yield ETF

The VYMI ETF (Vanguard International High Dividend Yield) is an actively managed fund that aims to surpass the average yield on international stocks while providing opportunities for growth of principal. With a minimum of 90% of its assets invested in dividend-paying stocks of established companies outside the United States, VYMI offers investors exposure to global markets.

VYMI's investment strategy relies on fundamental analysis to identify undervalued companies with potential for consistent dividend growth. The fund's diversified portfolio, managed by individual segments, allows for effective risk management.

As of April 30, 2024, VYMI boasts an average yield, according to Morningstar. Investors can also track VYMI's latest stock price and dividend history on platforms like TipRanks.

While past performance does not guarantee future results, VYMI's focus on high-quality international dividend-paying stocks may appeal to investors seeking income that exceeds the average yield of the broader market, along with potential long-term growth of principal.

VYMI - Portfolio and Asset Allocation

The VYMI ETF (Vanguard International High Dividend Yield) is a fund focusing on high dividend yield stocks across various sectors. The top holdings showcase a diverse mix of companies from different industries, including Toyota Motor Corp., Nestlé, Shell plc (UK), Novartis, Roche Holding, Hsbc Holdings Plc (UK), BHP Group Ltd, Siemens Ag (UK), TotalEnergies SE, and Vanguard Cash Management Market Liquidity Fund.

The sector breakdown further emphasizes the fund's diversification strategy, with investments spread across multiple sectors. Financials (35.24%) and Energy (10.05%) comprise the largest allocations, followed by Industrials (9.75%) and Consumer Cyclical (9.44%). This sector distribution suggests that the fund aims to balance income generation and growth potential while managing risk through exposure to various industries.

The presence of companies like Shell plc (UK), Hsbc Holdings Plc (UK), Siemens Ag (UK), TotalEnergies SE, and BHP Group Ltd among the top holdings indicates a focus on established, dividend-paying companies with strong market positions. These companies often have a history of consistent dividend payouts and are generally considered to be stable investments.

However, it's important to note that the fund also has exposure to sectors like Communication (4.63%) and Technology (3.36%), which can be more volatile and sensitive to market conditions. Nonetheless, the relatively lower allocations to these sectors suggest that the fund managers are cautious about overexposure to potentially risky areas.

Additional Information and Risks

In 2024, investors seeking above-average dividend yields turned their attention to the Vanguard International High Dividend Yield ETF (VYMI), an exchange-traded fund (ETF) designed to track the performance of the FTSE All-World ex US High Dividend Yield Index. This index encompasses stocks of companies located in developed and emerging markets, excluding the United States, characterized by high dividend yields.

The VYMI ETF offers investors exposure to a diversified portfolio of international stocks, with a focus on companies forecasted to have above-average dividend payouts. It seeks to mirror the performance of its benchmark index, providing investors with an opportunity to stay up to date with dividend data and track the latest stock prices.

Managed by the Vanguard Group, the fund's dividend payout history and dividend yield are proprietary to Morningstar, reflecting the fund’s commitment to transparency and performance tracking. While past performance may indicate the fund's ability to generate income, it's essential to note that dividends are not guaranteed, and the fund's performance may vary over time due to market fluctuations and management fees.

Investing in international markets through ETF shares like VYMI carries inherent risks, including currency fluctuations, emerging market volatility, and less developed legal frameworks. However, for investors seeking diversified exposure and potential dividend income from international stocks, VYMI offers a convenient and accessible investment option.
