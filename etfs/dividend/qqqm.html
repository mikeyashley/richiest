QQQM Overview - The Invesco NASDAQ 100 ETF

The Invesco NASDAQ 100 ETF (QQQM) is an equity exchange-traded fund designed to track the performance of the NASDAQ-100 Index, which comprises 100 of the largest non-financial companies listed on the NASDAQ stock exchange. QQQM focuses on providing investors with exposure to the technology, consumer discretionary, and healthcare sectors, among others, offering a diversified portfolio within the tech-heavy NASDAQ.

With a strategy that emphasizes index-based investing, QQQM aims to mirror the index's returns and capture the growth potential of leading companies in the market. Its quarterly payment frequency appeals to income-seeking investors interested in dividend-paying assets, although QQQM's primary focus is on capital appreciation through equity investments.

Investors benefit from global exposure through QQQM, as the ETF's holdings span across various geographic regions, providing diversification beyond domestic markets. The fund maintains a low expense ratio of 0.15%, making it cost-effective for long-term investors seeking exposure to large-cap tech and growth stocks.

As an ETF sponsored by Invesco, a reputable financial services firm, QQQM offers a convenient and efficient way for investors to access the growth potential of leading technology and innovation-driven companies in the NASDAQ-100 Index.

QQQM - Portfolio and Asset Allocation

The Invesco NASDAQ 100 ETF (QQQM) holds a diverse portfolio of leading companies in the technology, consumer discretionary, and healthcare sectors, among others. Microsoft (MSFT), with a weight of 8.63%, is a significant holding, reflecting QQQM's exposure to established tech giants. Similarly, Apple Inc (AAPL) and Nvidia Corporation (NVDA) contribute 8.07% and 6.37%, respectively, showcasing QQQM's focus on innovative companies driving market growth.

Amazon.Com, Inc. (AMZN) and Broadcom Inc. (AVGO) represent QQQM's exposure to e-commerce and semiconductor sectors, contributing 5.42% and 4.61%, respectively. Meta Platforms, Inc. (META), formerly Facebook, adds to QQQM's tech holdings with 4.61%. Alphabet Inc. Class A (GOOGL) and Alphabet Inc. Class C (GOOG) diversify QQQM's tech exposure, each around 2.80%.

Beyond tech, Costco Wholesale Corp. (COST) represents consumer defensive stocks in QQQM's portfolio at 2.49%, showcasing a balanced approach. Tesla Motors (TSLA), with 2.39%, adds volatility and growth potential to the mix.

Overall, QQQM's sector allocation reflects a strong emphasis on technology, accounting for 49.74% of the portfolio, followed by communication services at 15.86% and consumer cyclical at 15.35%. This allocation strategy aligns with QQQM's goal of capturing growth opportunities in innovative sectors while maintaining diversification.
