The Bottom Line Up Front

Quick take: FTQI is FTHI's tech-heavy sibling — a covered call ETF that owns Nasdaq-100 stocks and writes call options to generate monthly income. Because Nasdaq-100 stocks tend to have higher implied volatility, the options premiums are richer, which means FTQI can potentially generate even more income than its S&P 500 counterpart.

But this comes at a real cost: Nasdaq-100 stocks are growth-oriented and often don't pay big dividends themselves, so FTQI's yield comes almost entirely from option premiums. The 0.75% fee is high, and the upside cap can be particularly painful during technology-led bull runs. It's an income tool, not a growth vehicle.

Who Should Consider FTQI?

The Tech-Income Hybrid

You love tech stocks but want them to generate consistent monthly income rather than just sitting there waiting for appreciation. FTQI lets tech pay you every month via option premiums.

The Volatility Monetizer

You know tech is volatile. Instead of fighting it, you're collecting premiums from that volatility. FTQI turns the Nasdaq's wild swings into monthly income deposits.

The Portfolio Income Enhancer

Your portfolio is heavy on growth but light on income. FTQI adds monthly cash flow without forcing you to sell your growth allocation or load up on bonds.

FTQI in Action: Real-World Scenarios

  • Extracting Income From Tech Exposure: An investor already overweight in tech stocks via QQQ uses FTQI as a replacement — same underlying exposure, but with monthly income generated by option premiums on the position.

  • Income in a High-Volatility Environment: During periods of elevated market volatility (VIX above 20), FTQI's option premiums swell, generating above-average monthly distributions — exactly when income stability matters most.

The Richiest.com Final Verdict: Is FTQI Right For You?

FTQI is a specialized income tool for investors who want their tech exposure to generate monthly cash. The higher implied vol of Nasdaq stocks is a genuine premium source. But the 0.75% fee and severe upside cap mean FTQI should be a tactical, income-focused position — not a growth vehicle.

Use FTQI in income-focused portfolios where monthly cash flow matters more than maximum long-run returns. Size it modestly and ensure you have pure-growth tech exposure elsewhere in your portfolio.

FTQI Overview - The First Trust Nasdaq BuyWrite Income ETF

The First Trust Nasdaq BuyWrite Income ETF stands as an actively managed investment vehicle, strategically positioned to yield returns surpassing the average yield on U.S. stocks while fostering opportunities for consistent principal growth in line with prudent stock investing practices. With a commitment to allocating a significant portion of its assets to equity securities, FTQI predominantly targets companies with substantial market capitalizations, focusing primarily on dividend-paying stocks of established U.S. companies.

FTQI’s investment strategy employs a fundamental approach to identify attractively valued companies poised for sustained dividend growth. The fund’s portfolio is divided into segments managed by individual managers who make their own investment decisions, allowing for diversification.

While acknowledging the caveat that past performance does not guarantee future outcomes, FTQI’s focus on dividend-paying stocks of robust companies holds appeal for investors seeking income streams that outpace broader market averages, coupled with prospects for long-term capital appreciation.
