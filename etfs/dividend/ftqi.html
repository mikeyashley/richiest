Richiest’s Take

Quick take: FTQI applies a covered-call strategy to the Nasdaq-100, selling options on tech-heavy growth stocks to boost income—a high-yield twist on a typically low-dividend index.

FTQI (First Trust Nasdaq BuyWrite Income ETF) tracks the Cboe® Nasdaq-100 BuyWrite Index, which holds the Nasdaq-100 and sells at-the-money call options monthly. The strategy harvests option premiums from volatile tech names, creating a yield that far exceeds the index’s natural dividend payout.

FTQI works best for investors who want exposure to tech giants but also desire meaningful income, and are comfortable capping upside in exchange for that extra yield. If you seek full participation in Nasdaq-100 rallies or prefer dividends from company profits, consider standard tech or dividend-growth ETFs.

FTQI Explained: What It Is and Why It Matters

FTQI is an exchange-traded fund that enhances income from the Nasdaq-100 by applying a covered-call strategy. It sells call options on the index's constituents monthly, generating premiums that boost yield—transforming a typically low-dividend tech index into a higher-income vehicle.

The strategy comes with a tradeoff: while investors receive enhanced income, they sacrifice some upside potential during strong market rallies. This makes FTQI suitable for income-oriented investors who are comfortable limiting their participation in significant tech sector gains.

Key features of FTQI include:

  • Tech Exposure with Income: Gain exposure to major tech companies while receiving elevated yield through options premiums.
  • Volatility Harvesting: Profits from market volatility by selling options, which can provide downside cushion in volatile periods.
  • Liquidity: Trade throughout the day like a stock, with real-time pricing.
  • Transparency: Daily disclosure of holdings for full visibility.

The Richiest.com Final Verdict: Is FTQI Right For You?

FTQI is a compelling choice for investors who want exposure to the Nasdaq-100 with enhanced income, and are willing to accept limited upside potential. Its covered-call strategy makes it a unique tool in the dividend ETF space—especially appealing when seeking yield from a typically growth-oriented sector like technology.

However, if maximizing participation in tech rallies is your goal, or if you prefer traditional dividend income from company profits, standard Nasdaq-100 or dividend-growth ETFs may be better fits. Consider FTQI when income is a primary objective and you understand the tradeoff of capped gains.
