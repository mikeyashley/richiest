The Bottom Line Up Front

Quick take: JEPQ is JEPI's growth-oriented sibling — Nasdaq-100 quality stocks plus covered call premium for higher monthly income with more tech-driven upside potential.

JEPQ (JPMorgan Nasdaq Equity Premium Income ETF) applies the same covered call income strategy as JEPI but anchors to the Nasdaq-100 instead of the S&P 500. That means higher-growth tech holdings combined with option premium income — delivering even higher yields than JEPI while maintaining exposure to the world's most innovative companies.

JEPQ Explained: What It Is and Why It Matters

JEPQ selects stocks from the Nasdaq-100 universe — think Apple, Nvidia, Microsoft, Meta, and Alphabet — using a defensive tilt to reduce volatility. It then overlays ELNs (equity-linked notes) tied to Nasdaq-100 call options, collecting premium that gets distributed as monthly income.

Managed by JPMorgan Asset Management at a 0.35% expense ratio, JEPQ was launched in 2022 and has quickly grown to over $20 billion in assets. Because Nasdaq-100 options carry higher implied volatility than S&P 500 options, JEPQ typically generates higher monthly income than JEPI — often in the 8-11% range.

JEPQ Overview - Nasdaq Equity Premium Income ETF Dividend

The JPMorgan Nasdaq Equity Premium Income ETF (JEPQ) is a strategically managed fund designed to generate income surpassing the typical yield of U.S. equities while offering opportunities for principal growth consistent with prudent stock investing. This ETF allocates at least 90% of its portfolio to equity securities of companies with market capitalizations exceeding $4.0 billion, with a focus on established, dividend-paying firms based in the United States.

JEPQ's investment strategy employs a fundamental approach to select undervalued companies poised for sustained dividend growth. The fund's portfolio is segmented and managed by individual managers who exercise autonomy in their investment decisions, ensuring adequate diversification.

As of Apr 02, 2024, JEPQ reported an average yield of 1.54%, as documented by Morningstar. Investors also track JEPQ's stock price and dividend history on platforms such as TipRanks.

While past performance is not indicative of future results, JEPQ's emphasis on high-quality, dividend-paying stocks of established companies may attract investors seeking income that surpasses the broader market's average yield, coupled with potential long-term principal growth.
