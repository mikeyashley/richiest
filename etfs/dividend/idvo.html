The Bottom Line Up Front

Quick take: IDVO combines international dividend stocks with a covered-call overlay—a "global income plus" strategy that seeks higher yields than plain international dividend ETFs.

IDVO (Amplify International Enhanced Dividend Income ETF) tracks the S-Net International Equity Dividend Income Index, which selects high-dividend stocks from developed markets outside the U.S. and sells call options on a portion of the portfolio. The dual income sources aim to deliver a yield well above typical international equity funds.

IDVO works best for investors seeking diversified international income and willing to accept capped upside in exchange for enhanced yield. If you want full exposure to international equity returns or prefer dividends without option-based income, consider traditional international dividend ETFs.

IDVO Explained: International Income with an Edge

IDVO (Amplify International Enhanced Dividend Income ETF) is an exchange-traded fund that targets international dividend-paying stocks while enhancing yield through a covered-call strategy. Unlike traditional international equity funds, IDVO's approach aims to deliver higher income by writing call options on a portion of its portfolio.

This strategy combines two sources of income:

  • Dividend income: From stocks in developed markets outside the U.S. that have a history of paying consistent dividends
  • Option premium income: Generated by selling call options on selected holdings, providing additional yield enhancement

The fund is particularly suited for investors seeking diversified exposure to international income-generating companies while potentially capturing higher yields than conventional international equity funds. However, this comes with the trade-off of limited upside potential during strong market rallies.
