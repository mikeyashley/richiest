QQEW Explained: What It Is and Why It Matters

QQEW tracks the Nasdaq-100 Equal Weighted Index, holding the same growth-oriented tech and biotech companies as the famous Nasdaq-100 but with a crucial difference: every stock gets exactly 1% weight.

While QQQ allocates roughly 50% of its portfolio to Apple alone, QQEW limits Apple (and every other stock) to 1%. This equal-weight approach creates a fundamentally different risk profile:

  • Better diversification: No single stock dominates portfolio performance
  • Small-cap-like characteristics: Smaller Nasdaq-100 names get amplified relative to their market cap
  • Value tilt: Equal weighting inherently favors underperforming names (they get rebalanced up)
  • Active-like returns: The quarterly rebalancing creates a contrarian force that can outperform in volatile markets

First Trust launched QQEW in 2006, offering investors an alternative to traditional market-cap weighted tech exposure long before equal-weight strategies became popular.

Who Should Consider QQEW?

QQEW makes the most sense when you want Nasdaq-100 exposure without the extreme concentration of traditional market-cap weighted funds. It's designed for investors looking to complement their portfolio with a more balanced approach to growth.

Best for: investors who want Nasdaq-100 exposure with better diversification and contrarian rebalancing.
Not ideal for: investors who prefer traditional market-cap weighting or want pure exposure to mega-cap tech dominance.
Main tradeoff: you gain better diversification but face higher volatility than mega-cap weighted funds.

Growth Diversification Strategy

Use QQEW when you want to participate in the growth potential of Nasdaq-100 companies but with reduced concentration risk. The equal-weight approach provides exposure to the full breadth of the index without overexposure to any single name.

Portfolio Balance

Add QQEW to your portfolio if you already hold mega-cap heavy funds like QQQ. It provides a counterbalance by giving more weight to smaller Nasdaq-100 names while still capturing the growth potential of the technology sector.

Contrarian Exposure

Use QQEW if you believe in the long-term growth of technology but prefer a contrarian approach. The quarterly rebalancing mechanism systematically sells recent winners and buys recent losers, creating a disciplined contrarian strategy.

Common Use Cases

  • Balancing mega-cap exposure: If you already hold QQQ or similar funds, QQEW provides diversification across the same companies with a different weighting approach.
  • Value tilt within growth: Equal weighting inherently favors underperforming stocks, creating a value tilt within a growth portfolio.
  • Tactical growth allocation: Use QQEW when you expect smaller tech names to outperform mega-caps, or when you want to reduce concentration risk.
