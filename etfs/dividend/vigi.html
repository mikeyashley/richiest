VIGI Overview - The Vanguard International Dividend Appreciation ETF

The Vanguard International Dividend Appreciation ETF (VIGI) is designed to provide investors with exposure to international dividend-paying stocks, offering both income and potential for growth. The fund aims to track the performance of the Vanguard International Dividend Appreciation Index.

VIGI invests in equity securities of companies outside the United States with a track record of increasing dividends over time. By focusing on companies with strong fundamentals and consistent dividend growth, VIGI seeks to capture the benefits of dividend appreciation while providing diversification across international markets.

Investors can monitor VIGI's performance and dividend history on platforms like Morningstar and TipRanks. As of April 29, 2024, VIGI's average yield was 2.08%, reflecting its focus on dividend-paying stocks.

While past performance does not guarantee future results, VIGI's strategy may appeal to investors seeking income from international markets and potential long-term growth.

VIGI - Portfolio and Asset Allocation

The Vanguard International Dividend Appreciation ETF (VIGI) is a fund focusing on international dividend-paying stocks across various sectors. The top holdings reveal a diversified mix of companies from different industries, including healthcare (Novo Nordisk A/S, SAP), consumer staples (Nestlé), pharmaceuticals (Novartis), and technology (Roche Holding).

The sector breakdown further reinforces the fund's diversification strategy, with investments spread across multiple sectors. Healthcare (23.49%) and Consumer Defense (14.49%) constitute the largest allocations, followed by Financial Services (13.35%) and Industrials (11.10%). This allocation suggests that the fund aims to balance income generation and growth potential while managing risk through exposure to a variety of sectors.

The presence of companies like Novo Nordisk A/S, SAP, and Nestlé among the top holdings indicates a focus on established, dividend-paying companies with strong market positions. These companies often have a history of consistent dividend payouts and are generally considered to be more stable investments.

However, it's important to note that the fund also has exposure to sectors like Technology (9.77%) and Communication Services (8.01%), which can be more volatile and sensitive to market conditions. Nonetheless, the relatively lower allocations to these sectors suggest that the fund managers are cautious about overexposure to potentially risky areas.

Additional Information and Risks

The Vanguard International Dividend Appreciation ETF (VIGI) offers investors exposure to a diversified portfolio of common stocks of high-quality companies located in developed and emerging markets, excluding the U.S. As an ETF, VIGI attempts to replicate the target index by investing in a mix of foreign large growth stocks that exhibit strong dividend growth characteristics. The fund’s performance is measured against the S&P Global Ex-U.S. Dividend Growers Index, proprietary to Morningstar.

Investors should be aware that international markets can be substantially more volatile and less liquid than U.S. markets, posing risks such as foreign currency exchange fluctuations and geopolitical uncertainties. Additionally, stock investments are inherently volatile in price and may constitute investment advice.

It's important to note that past performance is not indicative of future results. While VIGI seeks to track the performance of its target index, management fees and other expenses may impact fund returns relative to the associated index.

Investors should stay up to date with VIGI's performance data, including its dividend yield, share price, and dividend history, available on platforms like Morningstar, Yahoo Finance, and Vanguard's website. As with any investment, individuals should carefully consider their investment objectives and consult with a financial advisor before buying or selling ETF shares.
