FTHI Explained: A Systematic Approach to Equity Income

FTHI (First Trust BuyWrite Income ETF) employs a covered call strategy on the S&P 500 to generate enhanced income. Rather than relying solely on dividend payments from constituent companies, FTHI supplements its yield by systematically selling call options on the index each month.

This approach has distinct characteristics that set it apart from traditional dividend ETFs:

  • Enhanced Income: Option premiums provide additional yield beyond standard dividend income.
  • Volatility Reduction: The income from options acts as a buffer during market downturns.
  • Upside Limitation: The fund's participation in market rallies is capped when call options are exercised.
  • Predictable Income Pattern: Monthly option sales create a consistent income-generating mechanism.

The strategy works by holding the S&P 500 constituents while simultaneously selling at-the-money call options each month. When markets are flat or declining, investors benefit from the option premium income. During strong rallies, the fund participates in gains only up to the strike price, with the rest going to the option buyer.

Methodology note: FTHI tracks the Cboe® S&P 500 BuyWrite Index, which holds the S&P 500 and sells at-the-money call options monthly with approximately one month to expiration. The index rebalances monthly, maintaining consistent exposure to the covered call strategy.

FTHI: Strategic Advantages and Limitations

The covered call strategy offers distinct benefits and drawbacks that investors should understand before investing.

Pros Cons
