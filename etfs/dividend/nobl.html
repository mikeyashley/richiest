The Bottom Line Up Front

Quick take: NOBL is best for investors seeking reliable dividend-growth exposure from S&P 500 blue chips. It is not a high-yield income fund.

NOBL (ProShares S&P 500 Dividend Aristocrats ETF) is the flagship Dividend Aristocrats ETF — the go-to fund for owning the S&P 500 companies that have raised dividends for 25+ consecutive years. Equal-weighted across ~65 qualifying companies, NOBL delivers consistent income growth, defensive characteristics, and blue-chip quality in a single, well-known package.

NOBL Explained: What It Is and Why It Matters

Want to own every S&P 500 company with a 25+ year dividend growth streak, all in one fund? NOBL has you covered.

NOBL tracks the S&P 500 Dividend Aristocrats Index, which requires member companies to have increased dividends for at least 25 consecutive years and be part of the S&P 500. The equal-weighting prevents mega-caps from dominating — every Aristocrat gets roughly the same piece of the pie.

Here's why investors consider NOBL:

  • Quality Screen: 25-year consecutive increases is a powerful filter — it eliminates most dividend traps automatically.
  • Equal Weighting: No single company dominates; avoids the mega-cap concentration of market-cap-weighted funds.
  • Defensive Characteristics: Aristocrats historically drawdown less than the S&P 500 in bear markets.

Managed by ProShares, one of the most recognized names in ETF product development.

NOBL Overview - ProShares S&P 500 Dividend Aristocrats ETF

The ProShares S&P 500 Dividend Aristocrats ETF (NOBL) is an ETF designed to track the performance of the S&P 500 Dividend Aristocrats Index, which consists of S&P 500 companies with a history of consistently increasing dividends for at least 25 consecutive years. NOBL aims to provide investors with exposure to high-quality dividend-paying stocks that have a track record of sustained dividend growth.

NOBL's strategy involves investing in companies that have demonstrated a commitment to returning capital to shareholders through dividends. By focusing on companies with a history of dividend growth, NOBL seeks to provide investors with income stability and potential for capital appreciation.

Investors interested in NOBL can track its performance and dividend history through various financial platforms such as Morningstar and TipRanks. While past performance is not indicative of future results, NOBL's approach may appeal to investors seeking exposure to dividend aristocrats within the S&P 500 for potential long-term income generation and growth.

NOBL - Portfolio and Asset Allocation

The ProShares S&P 500 Dividend Aristocrats ETF (NOBL) is a dividend-focused fund that invests in large-cap companies across various sectors. Its portfolio consists of a mix of companies representing different industries, including technology (e.g., Broadcom Inc., Microsoft Corp.), industrials (e.g., General Electric Co., RTX Corp., Carrier Global Corp.), healthcare (e.g., Abbott Laboratories), and consumer staples (e.g., Philip Morris International Inc., British American Tobacco PLC).

NOBL's asset allocation emphasizes diversification across sectors to balance income generation and growth potential while managing risk. The largest allocations include industrials (22.9%), information technology (19.3%), health care (14.3%), and financials (8.8%), reflecting the fund managers' strategy to provide exposure to various sectors.

The presence of established dividend-paying companies like Broadcom Inc., Microsoft Corp., and Philip Morris International Inc. among NOBL's top holdings underscores its focus on stable investments with consistent dividend payouts.

While NOBL also has exposure to sectors such as communication services (4.8%) and energy (3.7%), the fund managers maintain a cautious approach to mitigate potential volatility and sensitivity to market conditions.

Additional Information and Risks

Investing in NOBL and similar ETFs involves various considerations and risks. NOBL, ProShares S&P 500 Dividend Aristocrats ETF, tracks the performance of the S&P 500 Dividend Aristocrats Index, comprising companies with at least 25 consecutive years of increased dividend payments. While this strategy targets companies with stable dividend histories, it does not guarantee future performance. NOBL's portfolio includes top 10 holdings and index weights that may affect its performance.

NOBL's expense ratio, fund flow, and performance metrics are crucial factors to monitor. It seeks investment results that correspond to the index's performance, but volatility and market trends can impact returns. NOBL's affiliate, ProShares LLC, manages its operations, and investors should review its disclosures and regulatory filings with the US SEC.

ESG considerations are increasingly important, and NOBL's selection criteria do not explicitly prioritize environmental, social, and governance (ESG) factors. Investors interested in ESG-focused ETFs may need to explore other options.

Furthermore, past performance does not guarantee future results, and ETF investing carries risks, including market fluctuations and liquidity concerns. Investors should consult a tax professional for tax advice and consider their suitability for ETF investments.
