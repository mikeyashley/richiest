ITOT Overview - The iShares Core S&P Total U.S. Stock Market ETF

The iShares Core S&P Total US Stock Market ETF (ITOT) is a passively managed fund designed to track the performance of the S&P Total Market Index, which encompasses the entire U.S. stock market, including large-, mid-, small-, and micro-cap stocks. The fund allocates at least 90% of its assets to equity securities within the underlying index, providing comprehensive exposure to the total US stock market.

ITOT's investment approach focuses on mirroring the performance of the S&P Total Market Index by maintaining a diversified portfolio of U.S. stocks. The fund offers investors a low-cost, tax-efficient way to gain access to the entire U.S. equity market. Emphasizing passive management, ITOT uses index sampling techniques to enhance tracking and minimize expenses.

As of the latest data, ITOT had an expense ratio of 0.03%, according to iShares. Investors can monitor ITOT's real-time stock price and performance details on platforms like Morningstar and TipRanks.

While past performance is not indicative of future results, ITOT's extensive market coverage and low expense ratio make it attractive to investors seeking a thorough, cost-effective way to invest in the entire U.S. stock market.

ITOT - Portfolio and Asset Allocation

The iShares Core S&P Total US Stock Market ETF (ITOT) is a fund focusing on large-cap, dividend-paying stocks across various sectors. The top holdings reveal a diverse mix of companies from different industries, such as technology (Microsoft Corp., Broadcom Inc.), healthcare (Eli Lilly And Company), consumer cyclical (Amazon.Com, Inc.), communication services (Meta Platforms, Inc.), and financial (Berkshire Hathaway B).

The sector breakdown further underscores the fund's diversification strategy, with investments spread across multiple sectors. Technology (29.69%) and financial (13.04%) make up the largest allocations, followed by healthcare (12.06%) and consumer cyclical (11.20%). This sector distribution suggests that the fund managers seek to balance income generation and growth potential while managing risk through exposure to a variety of industries.

The presence of companies like Microsoft Corp., Broadcom Inc., and Amazon.Com, Inc. among the top holdings indicates a focus on established, dividend-paying companies with strong market positions. These companies often have a history of consistent dividend payouts and are generally considered to be more stable investments.

However, it's important to note that the fund also has exposure to sectors like communication services (8.56%) and energy (4.03%), which can be more volatile and sensitive to market conditions. Nonetheless, the relatively lower allocations to these sectors suggest that the fund managers are cautious about overexposure to potentially risky areas.
