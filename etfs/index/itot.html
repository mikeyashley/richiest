ITOT Explained: What It Is and Why It Matters

ITOT (iShares Core S&P Total US Stock Market ETF) is iShares' answer to Vanguard's VTI and Schwab's SCHB. It tracks the S&P Total US Stock Market Index, providing exposure to over 3,700 U.S. companies across all market capitalizations. ITOT was launched in 2010 as part of iShares' expansion into the low-cost ETF space.

Investors choose ITOT for:

  • Broad U.S. market exposure: Own the entire U.S. equity market with exposure to large-cap, mid-cap, and small-cap stocks.
  • Competitive cost: At 0.07%, ITOT offers solid value in the total market ETF category.
  • iShares platform integration: Part of BlackRock's iShares ETF family, with access to a comprehensive ETF lineup.
  • S&P indexing methodology: Uses S&P's indexing approach, which some investors prefer for its methodology and transparency.

BlackRock manages ITOT using a representative sampling strategy, holding a subset of securities that closely track the index's performance.

ITOT: The Good, The Bad, and The Steady

ITOT's main appeal is its S&P indexing methodology and position in iShares' comprehensive ETF lineup. But it's important to understand how it compares to VTI and SCHB before deciding.

Pros Cons
S&P indexing: ITOT uses S&P's methodology, which some investors prefer for its transparency and approach. Higher cost than VTI: VTI's 0.03% expense ratio is still lower than ITOT's 0.07%.
iShares platform: Part of BlackRock's comprehensive ETF lineup, offering access to a wide range of ETF options. U.S.-only exposure: If you want international diversification, you'll need to pair ITOT with an international ETF.
Broad market coverage: Holds over 3,700 U.S. stocks across all market capitalizations. Smaller AUM: At $40B+, ITOT is smaller than VTI's $200B+, which could theoretically affect liquidity in extreme market conditions.
Competitive expense ratio: At 0.07%, ITOT is reasonably priced for a total market ETF, though not the lowest available. No unique advantages: ITOT offers similar exposure to VTI and SCHB, so the decision comes down to indexing preference and platform.

Who Should Consider ITOT?

ITOT is ideal for investors who prefer S&P's indexing methodology or want to stay within iShares' ETF ecosystem. It works as a core holding for U.S. equity exposure or as part of a diversified portfolio.

Best for: iShares platform users, investors who prefer S&P indexing methodology, and cost-conscious investors seeking a total market option.
Not ideal for: investors who prioritize the absolute lowest cost (VTI's 0.03% is cheaper), those who want exposure to specific sectors, or traders looking for short-term opportunities.
Main tradeoff: you get S&P indexing methodology and access to iShares' comprehensive ETF lineup, but VTI offers lower fees and larger assets under management.

The Richiest.com Final Verdict: Is ITOT Right For You?

ITOT delivers solid U.S. total market exposure using S&P's indexing methodology at a competitive 0.07% expense ratio. If you prefer S&P's approach or want to stay within iShares' ETF ecosystem, ITOT is an excellent choice.
