SCHB Explained: What It Is and Why It Matters

SCHB (Schwab U.S. Broad Market ETF) is Charles Schwab's answer to Vanguard's VTI and iShares' ITOT. It tracks the Dow Jones U.S. Total Stock Market Index, providing exposure to over 4,000 U.S. companies across all market capitalizations. SCHB was launched in 2008 as Schwab's first ETF and has grown to over $60 billion in assets.

Investors choose SCHB for:

  • Broad U.S. market exposure: Own the entire U.S. equity market with exposure to large-cap, mid-cap, and small-cap stocks.
  • Low cost: At 0.06%, SCHB offers competitive expenses for a total market ETF.
  • Charles Schwab platform integration: No commission for online trades and free ETF revolving loans for Schwab account holders.
  • Passive management: Tracks its index with full replication, holding all securities in proportion to their market capitalization.

Schwab manages SCHB using a full replication strategy, ensuring tight tracking of its benchmark index.

Who Should Consider SCHB?

SCHB is ideal for Charles Schwab account holders who want a low-cost total market ETF integrated with their existing platform. It works as a core holding for U.S. equity exposure or as part of a diversified portfolio.

Best for: Schwab account holders, cost-sensitive investors who want a total market option, and those who prefer Schwab's platform and features.
Not ideal for: investors who prioritize the absolute lowest cost (VTI's 0.03% is cheaper), those who want exposure to specific sectors, or traders looking for short-term opportunities.
Main tradeoff: you get Schwab platform integration and competitive costs, but VTI offers lower fees and slightly larger assets under management.

The Richiest.com Final Verdict: Is SCHB Right For You?

SCHB delivers solid U.S. total market exposure at a competitive 0.06% expense ratio, with the added benefit of Charles Schwab platform integration. If you're already a Schwab customer and want to keep your investments within the Schwab ecosystem, SCHB is an excellent choice.

SCHB is not the right choice if you prioritize the absolute lowest cost (VTI's 0.03% is cheaper) or if you want exposure to international markets. For the lowest cost overall, VTI remains the leader. For Schwab customers, SCHB's platform benefits often outweigh the slightly higher expense ratio.
