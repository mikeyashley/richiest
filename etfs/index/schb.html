SCHB Overview - The Schwab U.S. Broad Market ETF

The Schwab U.S. Broad Market ETF (SCHB) is a passively managed fund that seeks to track the performance of the Dow Jones U.S. Broad Stock Market Index. This index encompasses a broad spectrum of the U.S. stock market, including large-cap, mid-cap, and small-cap stocks, representing over 2,500 publicly traded companies.

SCHB's investment strategy involves using a sampling technique to replicate the index. This approach ensures diversification across various sectors and industries, closely matching the index's risk and return characteristics.

As of May 15, 2024, SCHB had an expense ratio of 0.03%, making it one of the most cost-effective options available to investors. The fund's dividend yield is approximately 1.21%, with dividends paid on a quarterly basis. Investors can track SCHB's latest stock price and performance on platforms like Schwab and Morningstar.

While past performance does not guarantee future results, SCHB's broad market exposure, low expense ratio, and consistent dividend payments may appeal to investors seeking a diversified and cost-effective investment option with the potential for long-term growth of principal.

SCHB - Portfolio and Asset Allocation

The Schwab U.S. Broad Market ETF (SCHB) is a fund that focuses on large-cap U.S. stocks across various sectors. The top 10 holdings showcase a diversified mix of companies from different industries, including technology (Microsoft, Apple), consumer goods (Amazon), healthcare (Meta Platforms, Eli Lilly), and financials (Berkshire Hathaway).

The sector allocation further emphasizes the fund's diversification strategy, with investments spread across multiple sectors. Technology (29.72%) and financials (13.00%) constitute the largest allocations, followed by healthcare (11.99%) and consumer goods (11.20%). This distribution suggests that the fund aims to achieve a balance between growth potential and stability while managing risks through exposure to various sectors.

The inclusion of companies like Microsoft, Apple, and Amazon in the top holdings indicates an emphasis on established, leading companies with solid fundamentals. These companies typically have a track record of consistent performance and are viewed as relatively stable investments.

However, it's worth noting that the fund also holds positions in sectors such as communications services and energy, which can be more volatile and responsive to market conditions. Nevertheless, the relatively modest allocations to these sectors suggest a cautious approach to potential risks associated with them.
