The power of compound returns and starting your investment journey early

One of the most powerful lessons the Financial Samurai emphasizes is the importance of starting your investment journey early and allowing compound returns to work their magic over time. Compound returns refer to the snowball effect that occurs when your investment earnings generate additional earnings, which in turn generate even more earnings.

As the Financial Samurai notes, its time to start investing. "The earlier you start investing, the more time you have to benefit from the power of compound returns. Even small contributions made consistently over decades can grow into a substantial nest egg."

To illustrate this point, consider two investors: Investor A starts investing $500 per month at age 25, while Investor B waits until age 35 to begin investing the same amount. Assuming an annual return of 8%, Investor A would have over $1.3 million by age 65, while Investor B would have just over $560,000. This significant difference highlights the importance of starting early and giving your investments ample time to grow.
