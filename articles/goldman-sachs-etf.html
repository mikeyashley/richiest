Understanding the Concept of Exchange-Traded Funds

At its core, an ETF is a type of investment fund that is traded on stock exchanges, much like individual stocks. The key characteristic of ETFs is their ability to offer diversified exposure to a wide array of assets, sectors, or investment strategies within a single transaction. This not only simplifies the investment process but also provides a cost-efficient way to manage and diversify an investment portfolio.
