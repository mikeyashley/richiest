How to Open a Fidelity Brokerage Account

Steps to Open a Fidelity Brokerage Account

Opening a Fidelity brokerage account is a straightforward process. Follow these steps:

  • Visit Fidelity's Website: Go to the official Fidelity website and click on the "Open an Account" option.
  • Choose Account Type: Select the type of brokerage account you want to open. Options include individual accounts, joint accounts, and retirement accounts.
  • Complete the Application: Fill out the online application form with your personal information, including your Social Security number, employment details, and financial information.
  • Fund Your Account: Deposit funds into your new brokerage account. You can transfer money from your bank account or an existing investment account.
  • Review and Submit: Review your application for accuracy and submit it for approval.

You can also open an account in person at a Fidelity branch if you prefer.

Requirements for Opening a Fidelity Account

To open a Fidelity brokerage account, you will need the following documentation and information:

  • Social Security number or Tax Identification Number
  • Government-issued ID (e.g., driver’s license, passport)
  • Employment information
  • Bank account details for funding the account

Eligibility criteria may vary depending on the account type. For most standard brokerage accounts, there is no minimum investment required. However, certain account types, such as margin accounts, may have specific requirements.

What Are the Fees Associated with a Fidelity Brokerage Account?

Understanding Account Fees and Commissions

When considering a brokerage account, understanding the associated fees is crucial. Fidelity offers competitive pricing, but it’s important to be aware of the various fees that may apply. These include:

  • Account Maintenance Fees: Typically, Fidelity does not charge annual maintenance fees for standard brokerage accounts.
  • Commissions: Fidelity offers $0 commissions for online U.S. stock, ETF, and options trades.
  • Management Fees: Managed accounts and advisory services may have management fees based on the assets under management.
  • Other Fees: There may be fees for margin trading, wire transfers, and other specific services.

Exploring Fidelity's Commission-Free Trading Options

One of the significant advantages of investing with Fidelity is the availability of commission-free trading options. Fidelity offers $0 commission for online trades of U.S. stocks, ETFs, and options. This makes investing more accessible and cost-effective for many investors. However, it's important to note that other fees, such as option contract fees and expenses related to the underlying investments, may still apply.

By leveraging Fidelity's low-cost account features, investors can maximize their returns by minimizing trading costs. This commission-free structure is particularly beneficial for frequent traders and those looking to build a diversified portfolio without incurring high transaction costs.
